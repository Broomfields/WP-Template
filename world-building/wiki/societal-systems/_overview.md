# Societal Systems

The governments, economies, legal frameworks, class structures, and military organisations that shape daily life in [World Name]. Each entry covers how a system actually works — who it serves, who it doesn't, and the societies it holds together or grinds down.

Worth noting: the line between Societal Systems and Groups is "pattern vs people". A Societal System is an abstract structure — the feudal model, the class hierarchy, the legal code, the economic system. It's not a collection of people; it's the pattern that shapes how people live. A Group is a collection of people, organised or not, named or not — a family, an ethnic group, an army, a nation's population, a guild.
