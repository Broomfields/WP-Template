# Relationships

A relationship entry covers the story arc of a significant dynamic between two or more characters. The wiki might note that characters know each other; these files capture how that relationship actually plays out in this story — how it starts, how it evolves, what the turning points are, where it ends up. A relationship has its own shape that's distinct from either character's individual journey.

Only relationships with real story weight need an entry here. Not every character interaction — just the dynamics that drive or significantly affect the plot, or that carry thematic weight of their own.

## What an Entry Represents

One entry per significant relationship. Usually a pair of characters, occasionally a group dynamic. Covers the starting state, arc-by-arc evolution, key turning points, and ending state.

## How Entries Relate to the Rest of the System

- Entries link to each character's wiki entry via wikilinks in the `characters` frontmatter field
- Entries reference the arc in which the relationship first becomes significant via `arc_introduced`
- Story character files may reference relevant relationship entries in their notes
- The story dashboard surfaces all significant relationships

## Linking Convention

Use Obsidian wikilinks for each character in the `characters` array: `characters: ["[[character-a]]", "[[character-b]]"]`. These link to the wiki character entries, not to the story character files.
