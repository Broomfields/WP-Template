# Revelations

A revelation is any moment where significant information is disclosed — to the reader, to a character, or both at different times. Tracking them as individual entries maps the full information architecture of the story: what the reader knows versus what characters know, where knowledge gaps create tension or dramatic irony, and how understanding is sequenced across arcs and chapters.

This folder is the evolved form of a flat reader knowledge tracker. By making each revelation a queryable entry, the arc and story dashboards can surface a complete picture of when and how the reader's understanding builds — and where the reader is ahead of or behind the characters.

## What an Entry Represents

One entry per significant revelation. Covers what is revealed, to whom and when, any gap between when the reader learns versus when a character learns, and what setup needs to be in place for the reveal to land correctly.

## How Entries Relate to the Rest of the System

- Entries optionally link to a wiki entry (the concept or world-fact being revealed) via `wiki_entry`
- Entries reference an arc by ID and a chapter number to place them precisely in the story
- The arc dashboard surfaces revelations grouped by arc
- The root `reader-knowledge.md` provides the high-level story-wide inventory; this folder provides the detail

## Linking Convention

Use an Obsidian wikilink in `wiki_entry` if the revelation concerns a concept defined in the wiki (a magic system element, a historical fact, a character secret that has a wiki entry). Leave blank if the revelation is purely narrative with no wiki counterpart.
