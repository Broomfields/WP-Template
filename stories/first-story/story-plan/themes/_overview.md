# Themes

Themes are the ideas the story is actually *about* beneath its plot. Each theme file tracks one thematic thread: the question it poses, how it surfaces through characters and events, how it develops arc by arc, and how it resolves — or deliberately doesn't.

Tracking themes as separate entries makes it easier to ensure they're woven consistently across the story and that their development is intentional rather than accidental. It also makes it clear which characters are carrying which ideas, and surfaces any arc where a theme is being neglected or dropped.

## What an Entry Represents

One entry covers one thematic idea from introduction to resolution (or non-resolution). It identifies the core question the theme poses, which characters are its primary vehicles, and how the story's events test or develop it arc by arc.

## How Entries Relate to the Rest of the System

- Theme entries are largely self-contained within the story plan
- They reference characters and arcs by ID but aren't typically referenced back by other entry types
- The root structure document may note which themes are central to each act or phase of the story
